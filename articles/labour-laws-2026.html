7 Labour Laws Every Employee Must Know in 2026

Most employees don't know these rights until it's too late. Here's what every Indian professional should understand about labour laws, salary structures, PF, gratuity and workplace protection.

By Mihir Madhuvan • June 2026 • 12 Min Read

Why This Matters

India's labour law landscape changed dramatically after the implementation of the four Labour Codes. Yet most employees still don't understand how these laws affect their salary, PF, gratuity, notice period and workplace rights.

Whether you're a fresher, working professional or manager, understanding these rules can save you from costly mistakes and potential exploitation.

1. The 50% Basic Pay Rule

Under the Code on Wages, wages are required to constitute at least 50% of total remuneration.

Why this matters:

  • Higher PF contributions
  • Higher gratuity calculations
  • Better long-term retirement benefits
  • Reduced manipulation through excessive allowances

2. Full & Final Settlement Rights

Employees should receive salary dues, leave encashment and applicable benefits within the prescribed settlement timelines after exit.

Always keep:

  • Resignation acknowledgement
  • Last salary slips
  • Exit communication
  • Company clearance records

3. Gratuity Rights You Should Know

Gratuity is one of the most ignored employee benefits in India.

Formula:

(Last Drawn Wage × 15 × Years of Service) ÷ 26

For many employees, gratuity can amount to several lakhs by the time they leave an organisation.

4. PF Is Your Retirement Engine

The biggest mistake employees make is withdrawing PF every time they switch jobs.

  • Transfer PF instead of withdrawing
  • Check UAN regularly
  • Verify employer deposits
  • Update nominee details

5. POSH Protection at Workplace

Every organisation with 10+ employees must have an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC).

Employees should understand complaint procedures, confidentiality provisions and reporting mechanisms.

6. Notice Period Is a Two-Way Obligation

Notice periods generally apply to both employer and employee.

Always review your appointment letter before resigning.

7. Know Your Offer Letter

Most professionals sign offer letters without reading:

  • Notice period clause
  • Variable pay terms
  • Probation conditions
  • Bond agreements
  • PF and gratuity structure

Final Thoughts

The biggest advantage in today's corporate world is not just skill — it's awareness. Employees who understand labour laws make better career decisions, negotiate smarter and protect themselves from exploitation.
